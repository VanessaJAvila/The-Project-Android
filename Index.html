The History of the Android Mascot

You probably know that the Android operating system, maintained by Google, is one of the most widely used mobile operating systems worldwide. But did you know that its friendly mascot has a name and a very interesting story? Join us in this article to learn a lot about this little robot.

The First Version

The first attempt to create a mascot came in 2007 from a developer named Dan Morrill. He opened Inkscape (a free software for vector image editing) and created his own version of a robot. The goal was simply to personify the system for his team; there was no request from the company to create a mascot.

This first bizarre version was even named after its creator: they were the Dandroids.

A New Mascot Emerges

The idea of having a mascot kept evolving, and the task was passed on to a professional in the field. Russian illustrator Irina Blok, also a Google employee, took on the mission of representing the little robot in a more appealing way.

Irina's main idea was to represent everything graphically with a minimal number of strokes and a flat design. The illustration should also quickly generate identification with the viewer. Thus, the Bugdroid, the new Android mascot, was born.

The main inspiration for the features of the new Bugdroid came from those little figures that illustrate restroom doors to indicate the gender of each door. Legend has it that the artist was working at her desk in Google's office when she looked over at the restroom doors and had an epiphany: simplicity, cleanliness, and directness.

Discover More!

Did you know that each version of Android has been named after a delicious dessert, in alphabetical order, starting from version 1.5 up to 9.0?

  • 1.5 - Cupcake
  • 1.6 - Donut
  • 3.0 - Eclair
  • 2.2 - Froyo
  • 2.3 - Gingerbread
  • 3.0 - Honeycomb
  • 4.0 - Ice Cream Sandwich
  • 4.1 - Jelly Bean
  • 4.4 - KitKat
  • 5.0 - Lollipop
  • 6.0 - Marshmallow
  • 7.0 - Nougat
  • 8.0 - Oreo
  • 9.0 - Pie

Unfortunately, Android Q never saw the light of day as Google decided to put an end to this fun practice and started using numerical names, giving rise to Android 10.

Visit the Android History website to explore the sweet timeline of versions and discover what each one brought to the Android system.

And there you have it! We hope you've enjoyed this captivating glimpse into the world of the Android operating system and its endearing mascot. Get ready to embark on an extraordinary adventure with your Android-powered device!
